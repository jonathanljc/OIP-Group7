01

Divergent Thinking

Design Thinking

As part of our design thinking approach, we started by reframing the problem into actionable design questions using the "How Might We" (HMW) method. These HMW prompts helped us explore broad solution spaces without jumping to conclusions.

How might we count visitors accurately without disrupting their experience?

How might we implement visitor tracking in a way that respects the heritage status of the Kibble Palace?

How might we keep costs low while collecting meaningful visitor insights?

These questions guided our brainstorming and sketching exercises, resulting in a list of 6 initial concepts.

02

Convergent Analysis

Strategic Planning

During the Convergent Analysis phase, we systematically evaluated all brainstormed ideas to narrow down the most viable solutions.

Prioritization Matrix Methodology

To guide our decision-making, we employed a Prioritization Matrix — a strategic tool that mapped each idea based on:

User Value (Y-Axis)

The potential benefit or impact for visitors and stakeholders

Technical Complexity (X-Axis)

The effort, cost, and difficulty of implementation

This strategic tool allowed us to visualize trade-offs and highlight the most practical and impactful solutions.

03

Stakeholder Validation & Feedback

Critical Insight

When we presented our initial QR code scanning solution to The Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens, we received valuable feedback that fundamentally shaped our final approach.

Key Stakeholder Concerns Raised:

Children & Families

"Children often don't have their own smartphones, and families might find it cumbersome to use one device for multiple people."

Device Limitations

"Not all visitors carry smartphones, and some may have older devices without camera functionality or QR scanning capabilities."

Accessibility Concerns

"We need to ensure our solution is truly inclusive and doesn't exclude any visitor demographics from participating."

04

Solution Evolution: Hybrid Approach

Final Solution

Based on stakeholder feedback, we evolved our solution from a mobile-only QR code system to a comprehensive hybrid approach that ensures no visitor is excluded. A data dashboard will be used to display real-time visitor check-ins, track entry and exit timestamps, and provide summary statistics to aid operational planning and reporting.
