Concept Refinement

Divergent Thinking

Design Thinking

As part of our design thinking approach, we started by reframing the problem into actionable design questions using the "How Might We" (HMW) method. These HMW prompts helped us explore broad solution spaces without jumping to conclusions.

How might we count visitors accurately without disrupting their experience?

How might we implement visitor tracking in a way that respects the heritage status of the Kibble Palace?

How might we keep costs low while collecting meaningful visitor insights?

These questions guided our brainstorming and sketching exercises, resulting in a list of 6 initial concepts.

Convergent Analysis

Strategic Planning

During the Convergent Analysis phase, we systematically evaluated all brainstormed ideas to narrow down the most viable solutions. This process involved both qualitative evaluation and quantitative prioritization to ensure alignment with project goals and real-world constraints.

Key Evaluation Factors

Each solution was assessed against the following key evaluation factors:

Heritage Compliance

Does it preserve the structural and aesthetic integrity of the Kibble Palace?

Budget Feasibility

Can it be implemented with little to no cost?

Technical Viability

Is the technology stable, available, and maintainable?

Prioritization Matrix Methodology

These factors informed our use of a Prioritization Matrix, where each idea was mapped based on:

User Value (Y-Axis)

The potential benefit or impact for visitors and stakeholders

Technical Complexity (X-Axis)

The effort, cost, and difficulty of implementation

This strategic tool allowed us to visualize trade-offs and highlight the most practical and impactful solutions.

Stakeholder Validation & Feedback

Critical Insight

When we presented our initial QR code scanning solution to The Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens, we received valuable feedback that fundamentally shaped our final approach.

Key Stakeholder Concerns Raised:

Children & Families

"Children often don't have their own smartphones, and families might find it cumbersome to use one device for multiple people."

Device Limitations

"Not all visitors carry smartphones, and some may have older devices without camera functionality or QR scanning capabilities."

Accessibility Concerns

"We need to ensure our solution is truly inclusive and doesn't exclude any visitor demographics from participating."

Solution Evolution: Hybrid Approach

Final Solution

Based on stakeholder feedback, we evolved our solution from a mobile-only QR code system to a comprehensive hybrid approach that ensures no visitor is excluded.

✅ QR Code Check-In Using Mobile Phone

Position: High user value, low technical complexity

Description: Visitors can check in and out by scanning a QR code with their smartphones.

Why Explored: Easy to implement, minimal cost, no app download needed.

Value: High Complexity: Low
Top Candidate
⚠️ Bluetooth Beacon Tally Counter

Position: Moderate value, moderate complexity

Description: Detects when a visitor walks past a beaconed zone.

Why Explored: Passive method, no action required from visitors.

Issues: Hardware installation, signal accuracy, may not detect all footfall.

Value: Medium Complexity: Medium
Considered
⚠️ Passive Wi-Fi Signal Sensing

Position: Moderate value, high complexity

Description: Counts mobile devices pinging for Wi-Fi connections.

Why Explored: Non-intrusive and automated.

Issues: Not all visitors have Wi-Fi on, may overcount or undercount.

Value: Medium Complexity: High
Considered
⚠️ Heat Signature Counting with Thermal Cameras

Position: Moderate value, high complexity

Description: Uses thermal imaging to detect human body signatures.

Why Explored: Accurate headcount potential.

Issues: Expensive, requires discreet camera installation.

Value: Medium Complexity: High
Considered
⚠️ Entrance Mat Pressure Sensors

Position: Moderate value, high complexity

Description: Uses pressure-sensitive mats to count foot traffic.

Why Explored: Passive and can capture footfall automatically.

Issues: Hardware cost, durability, power and data connection concerns, complex analytics.

Value: Medium Complexity: High
Considered
❌ Manual Counting by Staff / Volunteers

Position: Low value, low technical complexity

Description: Staff or volunteers manually count visitors using handheld clickers or paper logs.

Why Explored: Simple to implement, no technology required, immediate deployment.

Issues: Labour-Intensive, Scalability, Cost & Coordination

Value: Low Complexity: Low
Rejected
