Problem Statement

Understanding the challenge at Kibble Palace

The Challenge

The Kibble Palace, a heritage-listed 19th-century glasshouse in Glasgow Botanic Gardens, attracts a wide range of local and international visitors but currently lacks a reliable, accurate, and low-cost method to track visitor footfall.

Previous Attempts

While previous attempts such as cellphone signal tracking were explored, these proved inaccurate and unreliable for gathering meaningful visitor data.

Critical Need

Accurate visitor data, including metrics like peak times, seasonal trends, and basic demographics, is crucial for supporting budget allocation decisions, especially as city council funding continues to decline due to competing priorities like education.

Solution Requirements

Discreet implementation
High accuracy
Comprehensive metrics
Demographics capture
Cost-effective
Heritage site compliant

Proposed Solution

QR Code & Kiosk-Based Visitor Tracking

Voluntary QR Code Check-In

Smartphone users can quickly scan QR codes at the entrance for instant check-in/out

Discreet Kiosk Station

Alternative interface for visitors without smartphones or those preferring physical interaction

Visual Analytics Dashboard

Data visualization for staff to monitor visitor patterns and trends

Key Features

No Structural Modifications

Respects heritage building constraints

Cost-Effective

Leverages existing low-cost or free technologies

Inclusive Design

Works for all visitor demographics

AR Integration

Augmented Reality feature enhance engagement and gently remind users to check out

Solution Justification

Why Our Approach is the Optimal Choice

Criterion QR & Kiosk Solution Benefit
Accuracy Requirement Enables timestamped check-in and check-out data, providing reliable metrics
Budget Constraint Utilizes existing low-cost or free technologies (e.g., QR codes, simple tablets), avoiding costly infrastructure or subscriptions.
Listed Building Compliance Fully non-invasive solution that respects the building’s listed status, no sensors, cameras, or modifications to heritage structures
Inclusivity Offers both mobile and physical interaction options, ensuring accessibility for all visitor types, with or without mobile phones

This balances user-centric design with feasibility and heritage constraints, while giving stakeholders a scalable base for visitor analytics.
