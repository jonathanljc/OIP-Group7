Storyboarding

User Journey

We began our prototyping process with comprehensive storyboarding to map out user journeys and identify key interaction points. This helped us understand the complete visitor experience and design touchpoints that would seamlessly integrate into their natural flow.

Critical Discovery: Check-Out Challenge

Storyboarding revealed that users might forget to check out after their visit. This poses a risk to exit data accuracy, which is critical for planning and budgeting.

To address this, and inspired by positive user attitudes toward digital features during our user interviews, we introduced an Augmented Reality (AR) component to boost engagement while subtly reminding users to check out.

Prototyping Process

3 Phases

Following our storyboarding phase, we adopted an iterative prototyping approach, progressing from low-fidelity paper prototypes to high-fidelity interactive demos. This methodology allowed us to test and refine concepts quickly while minimizing development costs.

01

Lo-Fi Paper Prototypes

We came out with Lo-Fi paper prototypes to rapidly explore multiple interface concepts without technical constraints. This allowed us to quickly test different layouts, interaction flows, and user interface elements before committing to digital development.

02

Hi-Fi Functional Prototypes

Then we developed Hi-Fi functional prototypes so that we could provide final validation of our design decisions. With realistic visuals, actual QR code generation, and functional interfaces, we could conduct comprehensive user testing and demonstrate the complete solution to stakeholders for final approval.

03

User Testing & Validation

Finally, we conducted comprehensive user testing with representatives from our identified user groups. This validation phase ensured our hybrid solution truly met the needs of all visitor demographics and provided valuable insights for final refinements.

Accessibility Validated

Both elderly visitors and families with children successfully used the hybrid system

Stakeholder Approval

Final solution met all heritage constraints and budget requirements

Technical Feasibility

Prototypes demonstrated reliable functionality across all target devices

Final Solution: Hybrid QR/Kiosk System with AR component

Delivered

Our final solution successfully addresses all identified user needs and constraints through a comprehensive hybrid approach that ensures inclusivity while maintaining the heritage integrity of the Kibble Palace.

QR Code Interface

Mobile-friendly scanning for tech-savvy visitors with smartphone access

  • No app download required
  • Instant check-in/check-out
  • Multi-language support

Kiosk Interface

Physical touchscreen for visitors without smartphones or those preferring traditional interfaces

  • Large, accessible buttons
  • Simple navigation
  • Group check-in support

AR Enhancement

Augmented reality component to increase engagement and remind users to check out

  • Interactive experience
  • Check-out reminders
  • Enhanced visitor engagement

Data Analytics

Comprehensive visitor tracking and reporting for operational planning

  • Real-time visitor counts
  • Historical data analysis
  • Budget planning insights
